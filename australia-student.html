Your Guide to Australian Student Visas

Dreaming of studying in Australia? This page is your ultimate guide for obtaining a student visa in Australia, offering insights into why Australia is an excellent choice for your studies, an overview of student visa types, the benefits of studying in Australia, top universities, tuition fees, application requirements and process, and opportunities after graduation.

Why Choose Australia for Your Studies?

  • Home to 38 world-renowned universities featured in the QS Rankings, promising exceptional education.
  • Opportunities for scholarships up to AUD 20,000, making higher education more accessible.
  • Competitive tuition rates for a world-class education experience.
  • Efficient visa processing to facilitate your educational journey.
  • Eligibility for a 4-year post-study work permit to jumpstart your career.
  • Pathways to permanent residency for qualified individuals.
  • Diverse course offerings and employment opportunities post-graduation.

Types of Australian Student Visas

Visa Type Description
Student visa (subclass 500) Allows full-time study, part-time work, family inclusion, and travel.
Student Guardian visa (subclass 590) For guardians accompanying students to Australia.
Training visa (subclass 407) For professional development and training purposes.

Visa Validity Based on Course Duration

  • For courses longer than 10 months ending in Nov/Dec: Visa extends until March 15 of the following year.
  • For courses over 10 months finishing between Jan-Oct: Visa validity extends for two months post-course completion.
  • For courses 10 months or shorter: Visa is valid for an additional month after the course ends.

Main Intakes for Australian Universities

  • Primary Intake: February
  • Secondary Intake: July
  • Some institutions may offer additional intakes in September and November.

Requirements for Australian Student Visa Application

  • Evidence of English proficiency through tests like IELTS or TOEFL.
  • An offer letter from an Australian institution.
  • Confirmation of Enrolment (CoE) for your chosen program.
  • Meeting the Genuine Temporary Entrant (GTE) requirement.
  • Proof of financial capability to cover tuition and living expenses.
  • Enrollment in Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC).
  • Meeting health and character requirements.

Application Process for an Australian Student Visa

  1. Check your eligibility against the visa requirements.
  2. Organize the necessary documentation for the application.
  3. Apply online through the official Australian immigration website.
  4. Wait for the decision on your visa application.
  5. Prepare for your journey to Australia upon visa approval.

Visa Fees and Processing Times

The base application charge for a Student visa (subclass 500) is AUD650, with additional charges for any accompanying family members. The typical processing time for an Australian student visa is around four weeks.

Opportunities After Graduation

  • Bachelor degrees now qualify for a 4-year post-study work permit, up from 2 years.
  • Master’s degrees will be eligible for a 5-year post-study work permit, increased from 3 years.
  • Doctoral qualifications can lead to a 6-year post-study work permit, an increase from 4 years.
