Canada Study Visa Guide: Your Path to Academic Excellence

Reasons to Choose Canada for Your Higher Studies

  • Recognized Global Education: Prestigious education recognized worldwide.
  • Affordable Education: Quality education without the exorbitant fees.
  • Immigrant-Friendly: Openness to immigration, enhancing prospects.
  • Vibrant Campus Atmosphere: Diverse student communities for growth.
  • Safe and Healthy Communities: Commitment to safety and health for students.
  • Work and Internship Opportunities: Post-Graduation Work Permit (PGWP) for valuable job experience.

Canada Study Visa Eligibility Criteria

  • Educational Background: Minimum of 50% from a recognized university.
  • Timely Graduation: Completion within the specified duration.
  • No more than 10 Backlogs: Ensures a consistent academic record.

Staying in Canada After Studies

To remain in Canada after studies, a course lasting at least 8 months is required. Courses shorter than 8 months do not qualify for a Post-Graduation Work Permit (PGWP).

Canada Student Visa Fees

Tuition fees range from CAD 7,000 to CAD 35,000 per year, varying by program and province.

Exploring Upcoming Intakes in Canada

  • Fall Semester (September): Major intake, offering numerous opportunities.
  • Winter Semester (January): Another entry point for students.
  • Summer Semester (April/May): Limited programs and colleges.

Applying Ahead for Success

Applying 6 to 9 months before the academic session commences is recommended for better admission and scholarship opportunities.

Exploring Work Authorization for Students and Spouses

  • For Student Applicants: Can work on-campus or off-campus for up to 20 hours per week.
  • For Spouses: Can apply for an open work permit, allowing them to work for Canadian employers.

Exploring Student Visa Requirements

  • Valid passport and educational documents.
  • Academic and employer references.
  • Statement of Purpose (SOP) and extracurricular achievement certificates.
  • Acceptance letter and proof of payment.
  • Financial funds proof and passport photographs.
  • Study permit and visa, English proficiency proof.

Canada Student Visa Process

  1. Research your options and select universities.
  2. Prepare for entrance exams and arrange finances.
  3. Apply to university and confirm admission.
  4. Get your student visa.

Canada Student Visa and Dependents

Foreign nationals holding a Canada student visa can bring their spouse and dependent children. The spouse can apply for an open work permit.
