Canada Work Visa: Your Gateway to Opportunities

Canada, known for its opportunities and quality of life, invites skilled immigrants to contribute to its workforce. With the assistance of top visa consultancies in Delhi, securing a work visa can be your pathway to a promising career in Canada.

What is the Canada Work Visa?

The Canada Work Visa is issued to eligible international individuals aiming to work in Canada for a specific time. Candidates require a job offer from a Canadian employer, who must obtain a Labor Market Impact Assessment (LMIA) to hire foreign skilled workers.

Benefits of the Canada Work Visa

  • Work under a designated employer in Canada
  • Opportunity to apply for Dependent Visas
  • Earn in dollars and experience world-class working conditions
  • Freedom to travel across Canada
  • Potential pathway to Permanent Residency

Eligibility Criteria for Working in Canada

To apply for a work permit, candidates need a valid job offer from a Canadian employer along with other requirements, including sufficient funds, no criminal record, and work with an eligible employer.

Types of Canadian Work Permits

  1. LMIA Required: Temporary Foreign Worker Program, Facilitated LMIA (Quebec), Global Talent Stream
  2. No LMIA but Job Offer Required: International Mobility Program, NAFTA Work Permits, etc.
  3. Open Work Permits: Post Graduation Work Permits, Spousal Sponsorships, International Experience Canada, etc.

Eligibility Requirements for Work Permit Outside Canada

Candidates must prove intent to leave Canada after work permit expiry, show sufficient funds, have no criminal or medical inadmissibility, and meet other specific requirements.

Documents Required for Canada Work Permit

  • Valid passport
  • Recent passport-sized photographs
  • Educational and work experience proof
  • Medical examination reports
  • Proof of sufficient funds
  • Application fee
  • Intent to return to home country after employment

How to Get a Canadian Work Permit?

Obtaining a Canadian work permit involves creating an Express Entry profile, indicating preference for Saskatchewan, and applying through the SINP portal. The process includes document submission, receiving provincial nomination, and applying for permanent residency.

Application Process

The process varies depending on the type of work permit. International students in Canada can work part-time and apply for a Post Graduation Work Permit (PGWP) after studies. Most work permits require a valid job offer and LMIA, while some categories allow application without these requirements.

Types of Work Permit

  1. Closed Work Permit: Tied to a specific employer.
  2. Open Work Permit: Allows work for any employer in Canada, with certain exceptions.
